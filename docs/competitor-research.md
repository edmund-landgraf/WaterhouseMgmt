# Competitor and Market Research

This document adapts the BrokerBizOps competitor research structure to Waterhouse's operating areas.

## Market Categories

Waterhouse competes across multiple service lanes:

- Manufactured home community management.
- Apartment and property management.
- Manufactured home dealership and infill coordination.
- Asphalt paving and repair.
- Real estate brokerage.

## Property Management Competitors

Typical competitors:

- Regional property management firms.
- Manufactured housing community operators.
- Apartment management companies.
- Local owner-operators with in-house teams.

Waterhouse positioning:

- Multi-line operating experience.
- Community-specific knowledge.
- Integrated management, sales, maintenance, and project coordination.
- Long-term manufactured home community focus.

## Manufactured Home Sales and Infill Competitors

Typical competitors:

- Manufactured home dealers.
- Community sales offices.
- Independent mobile home brokers.
- Factory-direct sales teams.

Waterhouse positioning:

- Home selection connected to lot readiness.
- Delivery, utility, setup, and occupancy coordination.
- Community-aware sales process.

## Asphalt and Paving Competitors

Typical competitors:

- Local paving contractors.
- General maintenance vendors.
- Civil site contractors.

Waterhouse positioning:

- Owner/operator understanding of community roads, parking areas, and disruption constraints.
- Practical scope paths: crack fill, slurry, grind-down, partial removal, full removal, grading, and resurfacing.

## Brokerage Competitors

Typical competitors:

- Residential agents.
- Commercial brokerage teams.
- Manufactured housing specialists.
- Park and apartment investment brokers.

Waterhouse positioning:

- Cross-over experience with manufactured homes, parks, apartments, land, and investment property.
- Ability to understand operations, not only listings.
