# Business Lines

Waterhouse is not a single narrow service company. The public site implies several distinct mini-businesses operating under one brand.

## 1. Property and Asset Management

Primary users:

- park owners
- apartment owners
- residents
- onsite managers
- regional managers
- maintenance teams
- vendors
- accounting staff

Core jobs:

- operate mobile home communities and apartment complexes
- fill vacancies
- screen applicants
- collect rent
- respond to maintenance issues
- coordinate onsite and regional management
- manage vendors and contractors
- inspect properties
- track budgets, expenses, and owner reporting
- stay compliant with local, state, and federal rules
- handle leases and tenant disputes
- protect and increase property value

Product implication:

This business line needs the strongest system backbone because it touches residents, managers, vendors, payments, accounting, documents, compliance, and ownership reporting.

## 2. Manufactured Home Dealership and Infill

Primary users:

- park owners
- home buyers
- sales staff
- home manufacturers
- transport vendors
- setup crews
- demolition/removal vendors
- financing partners

Core jobs:

- identify vacant or obsolete lots
- remove homes beyond repair
- dispose and recycle materials
- prepare lots for new homes
- select homes that fit utility, size, cost, and buyer requirements
- coordinate manufacturers
- plan delivery
- handle setup and finishing
- sell homes
- increase park occupancy

Product implication:

This business line behaves like a blend of dealership CRM, construction project management, logistics, financing workflow, and listing marketplace.

## 3. Asphalt Paving

Primary users:

- property owners
- Waterhouse estimating staff
- paving crews
- equipment operators
- material suppliers
- subcontractors
- accounting staff

Core jobs:

- intake quote requests
- assess pavement condition
- estimate repair or replacement options
- schedule work
- manage crews and equipment
- track materials
- document job progress
- handle disposal and recycling
- invoice and close out work

Product implication:

This business line is field-service and construction-heavy. It needs estimating, dispatch, job costing, crew tracking, photos, change orders, and equipment awareness.

## 4. Real Estate Brokerage

Primary users:

- buyers
- sellers
- investors
- brokerage staff
- lenders
- escrow/title contacts
- attorneys or advisors

Core jobs:

- manage buyer and seller leads
- list properties
- source investment opportunities
- coordinate transaction documents
- manage due diligence
- support negotiations and closing
- track investor relationships

Product implication:

This business line is pipeline and transaction-management heavy, with a need for document workflows, listings, due diligence checklists, and relationship tracking.

## 5. Community Marketing and Resident Experience

Primary users:

- prospective residents
- current residents
- onsite managers
- corporate marketing

Core jobs:

- publish community pages
- show amenities
- list phone numbers and addresses
- route inquiries
- schedule tours
- handle resident notices
- support community events and clubhouse amenities
- track reviews and reputation

Product implication:

This can be a shared module across property management and sales. The current site is mostly static marketing, but an internal platform could connect communities to available homes, resident portals, notices, and tour scheduling.
